Preface

We decided to format our user guide as an FAQ-style webpage which explains how a user might use each feature. We selected this format for a number of reasons.

First, the features involved in our project make use of standard patterns for common activities (e.g. account/project management, instrument creation/manipulation) as seen in other applications such as Docs, Photoshop, or Excalidraw; this means that most actions should be intuitive for most users. For this reason, we preferred to make information about the various features easy for users to find and reference, rather than attempting to predict which features users would require help with.

Second, the format of an FAQ page is common and well-understood. It will make it easy for users to quickly identify where they need to look for help within the guide.

Third, hosting the user guide in this way makes it easy for users to navigate and access from our site, without requiring excessive development time, allowing us to focus on developing our project.

User Account Instructions

Create account

Sign Up

As a new user of our web application, you may want to create your account first before starting your journey with us.

Click on the “Login” button at the upper right corner.

Then you will see the Login and Sign up page. Enter your email address and password here and click on the “Sign Up” button.

After you register successfully, we will automatically log you in. You will see your User Account page.

Sign Up Fail

If you see a pop up message that says “Email already exists” after clicking on “Sign Up”, you will need to sign up with another email address.

User Account details

Login

If you already have an account, you may enter your email address and password here, then click on the “Login” button.

If you login successfully, you will see your user account page.

Login Fail

If you see a pop up message that says “Invalid user email or password” after clicking on “Login”, don't panic. You entered the wrong email address or password for your account. You may want to try login again and type your email and password more carefully.

Logout

Click on the “Logout” button at the top to logout from your account.

Delete Account

Click on the “Delete My Account” button at the bottom of your account page to delete your account, and select “Yes” to confirm your decision.

Projects

Create Project

From your user account page, click on the “Create A New Project” button.

Then, it will lead you to your workspace for the new project.

Get Project List

From your user account page, click on the “Get My Projects” button.

Then, you will see all the projects you have created.

Share Project

Click on the “My Project” button for a project to share your workspace link to your friends via Facebook or Twitter. Don't worry, your friend accessing your workspace doesn't have the permission to save your project after modifying it.

After you click the "Share to Facebook" or "Share to Twitter" button, they will ask you to sign in first. Then you can share your workspace to your friends with one click.

Delete Project

Click on the “Delete” button to delete the project you want to remove, and select “Yes” to confirm.

Go to Project

Click on the “Go to Project” button to visit your workspace for that project. You can continue working on it from the place where you last saved your project.

Modify Project Name

Click on the name of your project and then you can change its name in the text box. Click somewhere else outside the text box to save your new name. Finally, click on the “Get My Projects” button to refresh your page and you can see your name is up to date.

Make a creation

New creation

From the User Account page, press the “Create A New Project” button

After the button is pressed a new creation will appear in the project list. In this example the new project is “NewCreation” with Project ID 11.

To access the new project, press the “Go To Project” button next to the project you wish to access. You will be taken to a screen that has your new project and you can start to build a creation. The new creation will start with a small example with a cannon and an instrument.

Add instruments and cannons

To add a new cannon or instrument, first select the note you want, then click on the radio button that corresponds to the object you wish to add. The radio buttons are located at the top of the page.

After the desired object is selected click in the scene for your creation anywhere in the light blue region. The object will appear and can be manipulated.

Move instruments and cannons

Ensure the select tool radio button from the top of the screen is selected and click on an instrument or cannon. A circle around the object will appear to show the object has been selected with a crosshair on the object. The inner circle is used for translation, just click and drag the center of the object to move the object around the screen. For rotation click the outer circle and drag to the desired angle.

Delete instruments and cannons

To delete an object, select the object and either press the “Delete Object” button below or use the Backspace or Delete button on the keyboard.

Sequencer usage

Schedule marbles

To schedule when marbles will be fired by the cannons, click one of the squares on the track below, and it will shoot a ball from every cannon associated with that track. The fired ball shows where the cannons will fire to make sure they hit the instruments you want.

Modify your track name

Click on the name of your track and then you can change its name in the text box. Click somewhere else outside the text box to save your new name temporarily, and click on Save button to submit your new track name to our system.

Play and pause

When the Play button is pressed the light gray squares will move along from left to right and fire all cannons with a dark gray square when the light gray reaches the dark gray. The Play button will loop through all of the squares on the track and play whatever beat you have created over and over.

Saving creation

Pushing the save objects button will save all the objects on the screen (except marbles) where they are in space and save the sequencer data to the database so next time you want to work on your creation, everything will be where you left it when you saved. You must allow it to finish saving or else the behavior of what was saved could be compromised.

Notice: Before you save your Sequencer group, click the Play button to make sure our system captures your latest version of scheduled marbles.
